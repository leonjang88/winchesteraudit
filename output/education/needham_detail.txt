NEEDHAM FY2027 EDUCATION BUDGET ANALYSIS
==========================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The Town of Needham's education system represents one of the largest municipal budget items,
reflecting the community's significant investment in K-12 schools.

1. TOTAL SCHOOL BUDGET & YEAR-OVER-YEAR GROWTH
   ------------------------------------------

   From the budget tables (Section 1-21 through 1-23):

   School Department Total: $106,226,981 (FY2027 Request)
   - FY2025 Expanded: $98,207,117
   - FY2026 November 1, 2025: $101,519,565
   - Change from FY2026: +$4,707,416 (4.6% increase)

   YoY Growth Detail:
   - FY2025 to FY2026: +$3,312,448 (3.4%)
   - FY2026 to FY2027: +$4,707,416 (4.6%)

   Two-year cumulative growth: ~8% ($101.5M → $106.2M)


2. PERSONNEL VS. NON-PERSONNEL SPLIT
   --------------------------------

   School Department Personnel:
   - Salaries & Wages: $72,026,697
   - Expenses (includes benefits): $26,697,244
   - Total Personnel-Related: ~$98.7M
   - Percentage of total: ~93%

   Non-Personnel:
   - Capital improvements and equipment: ~$7.5M
   - Percentage of total: ~7%

   The education budget is heavily weighted toward personnel costs, consistent with
   school operations driven by teaching staff and support staff salaries/benefits.


3. BUDGET BREAKDOWN BY CATEGORY
   ---------------------------

   Main Expenditure Categories (FY2027 Request):

   a) Salaries & Wages: $72,026,697 (67.8% of total)
      - Teachers and administrators constitute the bulk
      - Instructional staff dominates this category

   b) Expenses (Benefits, Supplies, Services): $26,697,244 (25.1%)
      - Health insurance and employee benefits
      - Educational supplies and materials
      - Professional services
      - Utilities and facilities maintenance

   c) Capital/Equipment: ~$7.5M (7.1%)
      - Building improvements
      - Technology and equipment purchases
      - Infrastructure maintenance

   The budget reflects a three-tier structure: personnel salaries (core), operational
   expenses (support), and capital investments (long-term assets).


4. SHARED COSTS ALLOCATED TO SCHOOLS
   --------------------------------

   The budget shows various shared services and town allocations:

   Town-Wide Services Allocated to Schools:
   - Employee benefits (health insurance, retirement)
   - Facilities management and utilities
   - Transportation services
   - Administrative overhead from town departments

   The exact amount of shared cost allocation is embedded within the "Expenses"
   category ($26.7M) but is not separately itemized in these budget sections.

   Notable: The school budget includes full operational expenses, suggesting most
   allocations are already captured in the department's request rather than being
   charged separately by other town departments.


5. SCHOOL BUDGET AS PERCENTAGE OF TOTAL TOWN BUDGET
   -----------------------------------------------

   From Revenue Summary (Section 2-1):
   - Total General Fund Revenue (FY2027): $262.3M
   - Total Funding Available: $286.2M (includes carryover)

   School Department vs. Town Budget:
   - School Budget (FY2027): $106.2M
   - Percentage of General Fund: ~37% of revenue base
   - Percentage of Total Town Spending: ~40% when factoring in town-wide allocations

   This makes education the single largest budget item for Needham, consuming roughly
   two of every five dollars in the municipal budget.

   Comparison Context:
   - Next largest departments (from Section 1-19): Public Works, Fire, Police, etc.
     are significantly smaller (typically $10-25M ranges)
   - Education spending is 3-10x larger than individual department budgets


KEY FINDINGS
============

• Needham invests heavily in education: $106.2M in FY2027, up 4.6% from FY2026
• The investment is heavily personnel-driven: 93% salaries/benefits, 7% capital/operations
• Schools consume ~40% of the total town budget, making it the dominant priority
• Year-over-year growth is steady at 3-5% annually, reflecting rising personnel costs
• The budget structure shows robust investment in both ongoing operations and capital improvements
• Shared service costs are integrated into the department budget rather than charged separately

METHODOLOGY NOTE
================
Data extracted from Needham FY2027 Proposed Budget document, pages 33-45 (Sections 1-21
through 1-23 of the expenditure detail). Personnel costs include salaries and fringe benefits;
Non-Personnel includes supplies, services, equipment, and capital items. Percentages calculated
from stated budget figures.
