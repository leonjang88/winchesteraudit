LEXINGTON PUBLIC SCHOOLS FY2027 EDUCATION BUDGET ANALYSIS
Program 1000: Education | Program 1100: Lexington Public Schools
========================================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
TL;DR: Lexington is spending $151.7M on 6,524 students (FY2027 budget). This is a 3.9% increase
over the previous year, with salary/benefits consuming ~$131.5M (87%) of operating costs. The
district faces enrollment decline (down 224 students YoY), consolidation pressures, and reliance
on Circuit Breaker reimbursements to maintain current staffing levels.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. TOTAL SCHOOL BUDGET & YEAR-OVER-YEAR GROWTH
------------------------------------------------------------------------

FY2027 School Budget Request:         $151,729,248
FY2026 Approved Budget:               $146,180,000
YoY Increase:                         $5,549,248 (+3.9%)

This represents the smallest increase Lexington has received in recent years. The budget request
originally sought 4.6% increase but was constrained by the revenue allocation model (74% of new
revenues go to schools).

Historical Growth Trend (LPS Operating Budget Annual % Increase):
  FY17: 6.11%
  FY18: 4.46%
  FY19: 6.35%
  FY20: 5.51%
  FY21: 3.82%
  FY22: 3.77%
  FY23: 4.39%
  FY24: 5.21%
  FY25: 4.16%
  FY26: 3.93%
  FY27: 3.90%

Analysis: Growth has slowed significantly from the 6%+ range (FY17-FY20) to under 4%. This is
attributed to revenue constraints and base budget allocation limitations under the Town's
revenue-sharing model.

KEY FINANCIAL DRIVERS & CONSTRAINTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. CIRCUIT BREAKER DEPENDENCY:
   The district received $7.9M in Circuit Breaker reimbursements (the highest in district
   history) to cover high-needs special education placements. The superintendent's message
   states: "We continue to rely on in-year Circuit Breaker funds, which creates budgetary risk."
   This is a red flag—the budget assumes continued high reimbursements that are not guaranteed.

2. ENROLLMENT DECLINE:
   - FY2026 Enrollment: 6,748 students (PK-12)
   - FY2027 Enrollment: 6,524 students (down 224 or -3.3%)
   - Per-pupil cost is rising ($23,196 in FY2027) even as enrollment falls

3. SALARY COST PRESSURE:
   Four of five union contracts are up for renegotiation in FY2027. The budget already accounts
   for negotiated increases. Any higher-than-budgeted settlements will require mid-year cuts.

4. STRATEGIC INITIATIVES:
   The superintendent references the "Bloom" design (voted December 2025) for a new high school,
   but this is a capital project separate from the operating budget.

SUMMARY FINDINGS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

HOW LEXINGTON SPENDS $151.7M ON EDUCATION:

1. Salaries & Wages (87%): $131.5M
   - 1,214 FTEs across teachers, administrators, support staff
   - Average salary not disclosed, but includes negotiated increases
   - Benefits (health, pension) embedded in payroll

2. Special Education Out-of-District (5.7%): $8.7M
   - Circuit Breaker covers ~$7.9M; district pays share of remainder
   - High-needs students requiring specialized placements

3. Transportation (2.7%): $4.0M
   - Regular & special education routes for 6,524 students

4. Technology & Admin (1.3%): ~$2.0M
   - Central office, HR, IT infrastructure

5. Utilities & Facilities (1.3%): ~$2.0M
   - Heat, electricity, water for 7 elementary, 2 middle, 1 high school, 1 pre-K

6. Supplies, Materials, Professional Services (0.8%): ~$1.3M
   - Instructional materials, contracted services, consulting

STRATEGIC POSITION:
Lexington is executing a "stabilization and transformation" strategy:
  - Stabilizing finances through Circuit Breaker reimbursements and careful staffing management
  - Transforming schools with the new high school design ("Bloom")
  - Managing enrollment decline (down 3.3%) while maintaining per-pupil quality
  - Negotiating labor costs with 4 of 5 unions expiring this fiscal year

RISK FACTORS:
  - Enrollment declining ~3% annually (reducing revenue while fixed costs remain)
  - Circuit Breaker reimbursements not guaranteed (represents 5% of budget cushion)
  - Labor cost pressure (4 contracts up for renegotiation in FY2027)
  - Capital needs (new high school, facility maintenance) separate from operating budget
  - Per-pupil spending rising (67% growth since 2008) in a declining enrollment scenario
