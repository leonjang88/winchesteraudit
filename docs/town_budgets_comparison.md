## Observations

1. **Lexington has the largest budget** (~$304M) despite not having the largest population — reflecting its high property values and service levels.
2. **Per capita spending varies widely** — from ~$3,500 (Medford/Melrose) to ~$8,800 (Lexington).
3. **Override activity** — Belmont ($8.4M, passed 2024) and Stoneham ($14.6M failed Apr 2025, $12.5M special election Dec 2025) both under Prop 2½ pressure.
4. **Melrose is notable for budget cuts** — $6.6M in service reductions despite a nominally growing budget.
5. **Winchester's fiscal challenges** — Special Town Meeting called in Jan 2026 suggests budget stress.

6. **Wellesley spends heavily** — $7,453 per capita, third highest after Lexington and Winchester, with nearly half going to schools.

### Key Observations — School Spending
1. **Wellesley spends the most per pupil** ($24,285) despite not having the largest school budget — fewer students, more money per head.
2. **Woburn is surprisingly high per-pupil** ($22,035) — schools are 52% of the total city budget with only 10.3% of the population being students.
3. **Lexington has both high per-pupil AND high enrollment share** — nearly 20% of residents are students.
4. **Stoneham and Melrose** are at the bottom per-pupil — both under fiscal stress with override needs.

### Key Takeaways — Full Category Analysis

1. **Education dominates everywhere** — 37-54% of budgets. Lexington spends $4,363/capita (42% more than Winchester). Medford/Melrose/Stoneham spend 45-53% less per capita on schools.
2. **Winchester leads on debt** — $653/capita, tied with Belmont ($651) and Wellesley ($635). That's 4-8x what Reading ($119), Woburn ($148), or Medford ($84) pay. Winchester is carrying Lynch School, flood mitigation, McCall renovation, and other major projects.
3. **Health insurance is the silent killer** — Lexington pays $1,200/capita (65% MORE than Winchester). Winchester's $727/capita is actually mid-pack. Every town is seeing 10-19% annual increases.
4. **Winchester has the most expensive library per capita** ($112) — Wellesley and Lexington are close behind (~$108-109). Stoneham and Melrose spend just $43/capita (62% less).
5. **Public Works gap is massive** — Winchester ($438/capita) is highest. Stoneham ($78) is 82% less, suggesting deferred maintenance.
6. **Wellesley's General Government is an outlier** — $563/capita, 88% more than Winchester. Includes high-cost items like $10.3M facilities management.
7. **Winchester is low on Health & Human Services** — $48/capita, below average. Wellesley ($78) and Reading ($72) invest significantly more in public health, COA, and veterans services.
8. **Arlington gets more bang per dollar on public safety** — Despite being the largest town (45K), they spend only $422/capita (31% less than Winchester). Economies of scale at work.
