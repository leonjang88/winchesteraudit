# Education Spending Analysis: Why Winchester Is Last

**Finding:** Winchester spends $14,915 per student (DLS FY24) — the lowest of all comp towns except Belmont ($14,212). The FY27 override budget of $76.3M raises this to $17,570/student, but still $3,430 below the comp average.

## Caveat: Classification Differences Distort Comparisons

**Not all "education" numbers are comparable.** Towns include different things:

| Town | What's in "Education" | Shared costs included? |
|------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Winchester** | School operating budget only | **No** — insurance, facilities, retirement budgeted separately |
| **Wellesley** | Direct ops + $45M shared costs (insurance, facilities, retirement) | **Yes** — inflates per-student by ~$11,500 |
| **Concord** | CPS + CCRSD (regional) + Minuteman | No, but regional school costs inflate total |
| **Lexington** | LPS + Minuteman | Partially — some shared expenses allocated |

### Adjusted Comparison (Adding School Insurance to Winchester)

Winchester's school employee health insurance ($9.3M) is budgeted under Benefits, not Education. If we add it:

| Measure | Raw | Adjusted |
|---------|-----|---------|
| Winchester education total | $76.3M | $85.6M |
| Per student | $17,570 | $19,721 |
| Rank | 8th of 9 | 7th of 9 |

Even adjusted, Winchester is still below the $21,000 comp average.

## FY27 Override Impact

| Scenario | Total | Per Student | Gap to Avg |
|---------|-------|------------|-----------|
| FY24 (DLS actual) | $64.7M | $14,915 | -$6,085 |
| FY27 (override) | $76.3M | $17,570 | -$3,430 |
| FY27 (adjusted + insurance) | $85.6M | $19,721 | -$1,279 |
| Comp average | — | $21,000 | — |
| To match Lexington | $92.2M | $21,242 | $0 |

The override closes about half the gap. Adding insurance allocation closes most of it. But to truly match Lexington would require $92.2M — $16M more than the FY27 override budget.

## What the Winchester FY27 School Budget Contains

| Category | Amount | % |
|----------|--------|---|
| Personnel (salaries + benefits within school budget) | $67,205,687 | 88% |
| Other Expenses (supplies, services, etc.) | $9,064,883 | 12% |
| **Total** | **$76,270,570** | **100%** |

The budget is 88% personnel — typical for education. The override scenarios:
- **Override passes (6.8% growth):** $76.3M → Level Services
- **5% Foundational (no override):** $73.9M → $1.4M in reductions, 12-16 FTE cuts
- **3.81% (no override):** $73.1M → $3.2M in reductions, 25-30 FTE cuts

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Winchester's education gap is real but smaller than DLS suggests** — classification differences inflate the apparent gap by ~$2-4K per student
2. **The FY27 override closes about half the gap** — from $14,915 to $17,570 per student
3. **Lexington is the best direct comp** — nearly identical student/pop ratio but spends $27.5M more on education
4. **Personnel is 88% of the school budget** — reducing per-student spend means reducing staff
5. **The constraint is Prop 2½, not wealth** — Winchester has $175M in override capacity ($20,951/household). The town can afford it; the question is whether voters will approve it.
