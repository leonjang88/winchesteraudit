# Why Is Winchester's DPW Budget So High?

**Finding:** Winchester allocates 8.3% of its budget to DPW — the highest of any comp town except Bedford (14.1%). Per square mile, Winchester's DPW costs $1.9M vs the comp average of $1.0M.

---

## The Short Answer: Facilities Are Buried in DPW

Winchester's DPW includes **building maintenance and custodial services** that other towns budget separately under a Facilities department. This inflates Winchester's DPW line by ~$2.4M.

| Item | Amount | What It Is |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Custodians | $1,906,122 | Building maintenance staff for all town buildings |
| R&M Bldg & Struct | $540,000 | Building repairs across 15+ town facilities |
| **Subtotal** | **$2,446,122** | **Costs other towns put in "Facilities"** |

**If you subtract these, Winchester's DPW drops from $9.9M to $7.5M** — still high per sq mile but much closer to the pack.

---

## How Other Towns Handle This

| Town | Facilities Approach | DPW % of Budget |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Winchester** | Rolled into DPW | **8.3%** |
| **Concord** | Separate "Facilities Administration" (pg 362) | 4.1% |
| **Lexington** | "Public Facilities" under Shared Expenses (Section IV) | 3.7% |
| **Belmont** | "Public Facilities" under Shared Services (Section IV) | 5.5% |
| **Needham** | Building Maintenance is a separate DPW division but broken out | 4.5% |

| Needham | $9,491,211 | 12.3 | $771,643 | Already separate |

> Even after removing facilities costs, Winchester's DPW per sq mile ($1.25M) is still above Lexington ($899K) and Concord ($307K). The remaining gap is driven by:
> 1. **Small geographic footprint** (6 sq mi) — fixed costs spread over fewer miles
> 2. **Solid waste** ($971K disposal + $385K recycling = $1.36M) — some towns use enterprise funds for this
> 3. **Overtime** ($546K) — 9% of DPW personnel costs

---

## Recommendations for Further Investigation

1. **Separate facilities budgeting** — Move custodians and building R&M to a Facilities line item for cleaner DPW comparison
2. **Solid waste** — Is $1.36M for disposal + recycling competitive? Compare per-household costs with comp towns
3. **Overtime** — $546K (9% of personnel) is worth benchmarking against comp towns
4. **Enterprise fund treatment** — Some towns fund solid waste through enterprise funds (user fees) rather than general fund (tax levy)
